## Slide-by-slide camera & framing rules

### 01 · COVER (the anchor)
- 16:9 full-bleed photograph **OR** 4-tile grid (2×2). Choose 4-tile grid only when no single frame can carry the heart-hit alone.
- The image must contain a *micro-detail* that hooks the eye in 0.5 sec: a single hand, a single object, a single beam of light.
- Brand lock-up `NN · BRAND` — top-left, thin serif, ≤ 18 px on the page. Tagline ≤ 7 words, lowercase italic, bottom-left.
- Light: time-of-day chosen from the city's actual best moment (Lisbon morning, Reykjavík blue hour, Cairo dawn, Warsaw 7 a.m.).

### 02 · ABOUT (the second sentence)
- 16:9 photograph that *continues* the cover — not the same scene. If cover is exterior, this is interior; if cover is macro, this is wide.
- 2–3 short lines of body copy, set on the slide as a thin column max-width 36 ch, bottom-left or top-right.
- The photo must *associate* immediately with the business direction (you read it before the words).

### 03 · AXONOMETRIC (the spatial seed)
- 30° isometric, roof off, watercolour line illustration on warm cream paper.
- Header: `NN · BRAND — City · District · m²`. Page mark `03 / 25`.
- Inside: every functional zone labelled **A · B · C · D …** with thin leader lines and short captions.
- Right side: typographic block with room dimensions (W × D) and total m².
- Bottom legend: A/B/C/D… expanded names.
- Right-bottom: full HEX palette list (5 colors) in tiny mono.
- Centre-bottom: italic serif manifest line (≤ 14 words).
- Bottom edge: 5-color palette strip edge-to-edge.

### 04 · VIEW 1 · entry group
- Camera position: 6 m in front of the door, 1.6 m height, slight upward tilt.
- Photographic render. Daylight matching the cover.
- Must show: facade signage (small, brass), door, immediate context (cobblestone, planter, threshold).
- No people front-facing; back-facing silhouette acceptable.

### 05 · VIEW 2 · from the door
- Camera position: at the threshold, 1.65 m height, looking dead ahead into the room.
- 28 mm wide, deep depth-of-field — back wall must be readable.
- Reception/entry zone foreground, main work zone middle, far wall in focus.

### 06 · VIEW 3 · back wall to door
- Camera position: deepest point of the room, 1.5 m height, looking back toward the entrance.
- Reverse of slide 05. Same lighting, same time of day.
- The eye should travel toward the door — anchor the gaze.

### 07 · VIEW 4 · right side wall
- Camera position: standing at the right wall (looking from door), 1.6 m height, axis perpendicular to slide 05.
- Shows the long axis of the room — left-to-right composition, bench/wall on far side, central activity in middle.

### 08 · MATERIALS (5 samples)
- 5 horizontal swatch photographs, equal panels.
- Each panel: a real material sample photographed at macro distance (raw oak grain, brushed brass, lime-washed plaster, linen weave, terrazzo dot).
- Caption under each: *material name + ≤ 5-word use*. Example: "raw oak — bench surface", "brushed brass — taps and pulls".

### 09 · DECOR (materials in life)
- One real moment from the room where 2–3 materials live together.
- Camera macro/wide hybrid — a corner, a shelf, a sink-and-towel vignette.
- 1 italic caption ≤ 12 words.

### 10 · THE RITUAL (the move)
- Macro photograph of the signature gesture **in motion**.
- Hands and one tool and one material in sharp focus.
- Italic line on the slide describing the ritual in present tense, ≤ 14 words.
- The ritual must be pre-thought and consistent across slides (the same gesture seen later in workspace photos).

### 11 · THE CRAFTS (finished things, no humans)
- Still-life of finished pieces — 3 to 7 objects, asymmetric editorial composition.
- Visible evidence of being *handmade* (slight irregularities, fingerprints in clay, hand-stitch tension).
- Neutral surface — oak, plaster, linen — no studio sweep.

### 12 · WORKSPACE A (front)
- Wide photograph of the actual work-station, frontal.
- Real, juicy, ergonomic — every visible item must be a real product (real espresso lever, real awl, real chair).
- Light from the window the axonometric prescribes.

### 13 · WORKSPACE B (perpendicular)
- Same workspace, opposite or perpendicular angle.
- Confirms reality — same surfaces, same lighting, different vantage.

## Order of operations (cache-first)

1. **Write the story in cache** — full 13-slide narrative as plain prose,
   slide by slide, before any image is generated. Each slide must read like
   the next sentence of the previous one.
2. **Slide 03 axonometric** is built next — it locks geometry for slides 04–07.
3. **Slides 04, 05, 06, 07** are generated *with reference to the axonometric*
   so the four angles describe the same room.
4. **Materials → Decor → Ritual → Crafts → Workspace** flow from the now-fixed
   spatial reality.
5. **Cover (01) and About (02) are generated last** — when the rest of the
   visual world exists, the cover becomes a true distillation, not a guess.

---

## Carry-overs (do not break)

- 16:9 always.
- Strict palette per concept (5 hex), no other colors.
- No stock smiles, no logos other than `NN · BRAND`, no emoji in copy.
- Editorial tone (Kinfolk × Apartamento × Cereal), tihi-luxury.
- Numbers that ever appear are benchmarks, never promises.
