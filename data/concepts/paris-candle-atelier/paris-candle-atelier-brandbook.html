The Concept

Melt, pour,
sell — in one window.

Parisian candle retail is a crowded, polished market ruled by the big maisons: Diptyque, Trudon, Astier de Villatte. Each sells the idea of craft but pours behind closed doors in factories at the edge of the city. Paris Candle Atelier inverts the equation — the pour happens in the window, visible from the pavement, with an apron-wearing chandelier at a double-burner hot plate and the smell of molten beeswax drifting to passers-by.

The 14 m² model is the whole point. No back-of-house storage. No fulfilment centre. Inventory exists only as raw wax, raw wicks, raw vessels; the candle on the shelf is the candle a customer watched poured yesterday afternoon. Authenticity is not marketed — it is watched.

The Window Rule

The pour happens between 10:00 and 18:00, Tuesday through Saturday, every week. If the window is dark, the atelier is closed. Never pour in the back. Never pre-make for stock.

USPs & Positioning

Why this wins.

Five defensible advantages over the average Parisian candle boutique.

  1. Pouring in the window, every day. The manufactory IS the shop. No fulfilment centre, no warehouse, no mystery.
  2. Four scents only. Cire (beeswax), Figuier, Tabac-Vanille, Marais. Rotate never. The constraint is the brand.
  3. Hand-poured beeswax from Île-de-France apiaries. Direct traceability to five named beekeepers in the 60 km ring around Paris.
  4. €12,400 to open, 5 weeks from lease signature to first pour. Modular hot plate, modular counter, no plumbing commitment.
  5. Zero-waste refill programme. Empty vessels returned to the atelier are re-poured for 40% off — turning regulars into a closed loop.

Positioning statement

For Parisian stylists, design-tourists, and Marais office workers who want a candle they can name a maker for, Paris Candle Atelier is the 14 m² Marais window whose daily hand-pour turns a purchase into a visible ritual.

Pattern Library

The four-wick grid.

The brand pattern is a single idea: four amber circles in a 2×2 grid, each circle a scent. Used on sleeves, wax-seal stickers, the floor decal at the window, and the Instagram grid marker. Never more than four circles. Never fewer.

Rules

2 columns × 2 rows. Circle diameter = 8% of surface width. Space between circles = ¾ diameter. Circles represent the four scents, always in order: Cire, Figuier, Tabac-Vanille, Marais. The order never changes — it is the recipe reading order.

Registration · France

Legal setup in 15 days.

France streamlined its micro-entreprise process in 2024. Registration via INPI is fully online and free; the first three months of activation cost under €300 in fixed fees.

1. Micro-entreprise

  • Legal form: Micro-entreprise (EI simplified)
  • Portal: formalites.entreprises.gouv.fr (INPI)
  • Registration fee: €0
  • Time: 7–10 business days
  • Required: CNI / passport + proof of address

2. SIRET + URSSAF

  • SIRET issued by INSEE automatically, 3 days
  • Code APE: 20.42Z (cosmetics & toiletries mfg)
  • URSSAF quarterly at 12.8% of revenue
  • TVA: exempt under €85,800/year turnover

3. Mairie + ERP

  • Déclaration d'ouverture at Mairie du 3e: free
  • ERP classification (5e catégorie): €0
  • Enseigne (sign) permit: €180 + 0.5% tax
  • Respect of voisinage hours for pouring

4. Insurance + CCI

  • RC Pro (liability): MAAF €240/year
  • Assurance locaux: AXA Pro €180/year
  • CCI Paris Île-de-France free support
  • Artisan registration at CMA: €130

Total legal + registration costs: ~€650 (CMA €130 + enseigne €180 + first-year insurances €420 + misc €80). URSSAF is a running revenue-based cost, not setup.
