The Concept

Pasta, rolled
this morning.

Florence has two kinds of pasta: €4 microwave-slop in tourist traps near the Duomo, and €24 plates in white-tablecloth trattorie that take 90 minutes. There is nothing in between — a proper, hand-rolled plate of cacio e pepe served fast, at a counter, for €12.

Nonna's 12 fills that gap with a disciplined 8-primi menu, twelve walnut stools, and the same 00 flour the Michelin places buy from Mulino Marino. The format is deliberately small (18 m²) because Oltrarno rent is brutal: drop below 20 m² and you cut rent by 55%.

The Nonna Rule

Every morning at 7am, the pasta is rolled by hand on a marble board — tagliatelle, pappardelle, pici, ravioli. By 11:30 the counter is open. By 22:00 the day's pasta is gone and the shutters are down. No freezer pasta. No second service next day. If it's not made this morning, it's not on the menu.

USPs & Positioning

Why this wins.

Five defensible advantages over both the white-tablecloth trattorie and the Duomo tourist traps.

  1. Rolled by hand, every morning. Customers watch the dough go through the Imperia before their plate arrives. No other €12 pasta in Florence does this.
  2. Mulino Marino 00 flour. Same mill that supplies Il Santo Bevitore and Il Palagio. €12 plate, three-star ingredients.
  3. 8 primi, no pizza, no tiramisù jars. Menu cost control is total. Staff training takes four days, not four weeks.
  4. 18 m² footprint. Rent drops 55% below 20 m² in Oltrarno. Closes the break-even gap that kills most Florence pasta concepts.
  5. Counter-only, 12 stools. No waiters, no tables, no 3-turn anxiety. 55% labor saving vs a sit-down trattoria.

Pattern Library

Wheat & fork.

One monogram, one stamp, zero decorative pattern. The Tuscan rule: if you can avoid a pattern, avoid it. Cream plaster + terracotta apron + brass bell = pattern enough. The only repeating marks allowed are the wheat-and-fork stamp and the numbered table of primi.

Monogram is used at 25 mm on paper (business cards, menu header, napkin stamp), 80 mm in enamel (door pull), and 400 mm in hand-painted signage (shopfront). Never stretched, never rotated, never placed on a photograph.

Registration · Italy

Legal setup in 3 weeks.

Italy is paperwork-heavy but manageable. Partita IVA in 24 hours, Camera di Commercio within a week, SCIA food-service filing via SUAP, HACCP plan on site.

1. Partita IVA + Regime Forfettario

  • Apply via Agenzia delle Entrate (free, 24 h)
  • Regime forfettario: 5% tax first 5 years
  • ATECO code: 56.10.11 (ristorazione con somministrazione)
  • Revenue cap: €85 000/yr for the flat regime
  • Required: codice fiscale, SPID

2. Camera di Commercio Firenze

  • Register as impresa individuale via ComUnica
  • Fee: €125 initial + €88/yr
  • Time: 5–7 working days
  • Requires commercialista sign-off (~€300/yr)

3. SCIA + HACCP

  • SCIA filed via SUAP portal (Comune di Firenze)
  • Zero wait — operational on submission
  • HACCP plan + manual on premises at all times
  • ASL inspection within 60 days of opening
  • Staff HACCP course: €40/person, online

4. Insurance & Social

  • INPS gestione commercianti: ~€4 000/yr fixed (minimum)
  • INAIL: automatic on registration
  • RC attività (liability): ~€35/mo
  • Incendio + furto (fire/theft): ~€25/mo

Total legal + registration costs: €500 (CCIAA fees ~€125, SCIA €0, HACCP course + plan €180, first-quarter insurance ~€195)
