01 · Executive Summary

The shop in one page

NONNA'S 12 is a micro-trattoria with exactly twelve seats, one hand-rolled pasta menu, and one human — a nonna — doing all the cooking in plain sight. It is not a restaurant. It is a ritual, reopened.

Why it works. 18 m² in Florence's historic centre costs €650–€850/month — inside reach. Twelve seats × two sittings × €28 = €672/day at full capacity. You only need 55 % occupancy to clear overheads. Every plate is pasta. One chef. One menu. Zero waste theatre.

02 · The Concept

One nonna, one menu

Florence has 400 trattorias. None of them is this small, and none of them says "no" this often. NONNA'S 12 is the opposite of a menu with 40 dishes. It's a menu with 3.

The daily offer

Every morning the nonna decides. Two pastas (one with meat, one without) and one dolce. Whatever is best at the market that day. Written on a single chalkboard. When the pasta runs out, the door closes. People queue for this.

The space

Four square tables for two, two banquettes for two, one stool at the pass so one solo guest can watch the rolling. Terracotta tiled floor, whitewashed walls, copper pans overhead, a single wooden door, a single window. No music from speakers — the kitchen is the soundtrack.

The ritual

Guests book for a 90-minute slot. Two sittings per night: 19:00 and 21:00. Lunch Thursday–Sunday only. Closed Monday. The nonna is on her feet 5 days a week; the 6th she rests or teaches a private pasta class for €120/head (4 seats, pure margin).

04 · Unique Selling Points

Why twelve beats fifty

  • One chef, one menu. No line cooks, no waste, no "86 the special." Labour cost collapses from 35 % to 18 %.
  • Reservations only. You know exactly how much pasta to make tomorrow. Food waste drops to under 3 %.
  • 18 m² means €750/month rent. A 60-seat trattoria in the same street pays €4,200. You open on a dishwasher's salary.
  • The nonna is the brand. She doesn't need a marketing budget. She is the marketing budget.
  • Pasta class as second revenue. One morning a week, 4 seats × €120 = €480 with near-zero COGS. That's rent money.
  • Scarcity pricing. 12 seats × 14 slots/week = 168 dinners. Demand always exceeds supply. You never discount.
05 · Brand Identity

The voice

Warm. Certain. Slightly old-fashioned. Never apologetic about being small. The brand speaks like a grandmother who has decided, once and for all, how the pasta should taste.

Tone in three words

Handmade. Decided. Unhurried.

Words we use

"Fatto a mano." "Oggi." "Solo dodici." "Chiuso." "Prenotazione obbligatoria." "Grazie, tornate presto."

Words we never use

"Authentic." "Concept." "Vibe." "Menu option." "Fusion." "Modern twist." "Upscale." "Dining experience."

07 · Typography

Two faces, nothing more

Nonna's Dodici
Body · DM Sans Regular
Fresh pasta. Rolled this morning. Served at seven and nine. When it's gone, it's gone — just like nonna's house growing up.

Rules

Display is always italic. Body is always upright. Menu items in all caps. Prices right-aligned. No third typeface ever — if you need emphasis, use italic or size, not a new font.

08 · Pattern Library

The checker

One pattern. One use. The red-and-white checker tablecloth is the only ornament the brand owns. It appears on the tablecloths, the napkins, the awning, and nowhere else.

Rules

Checker squares are 20 mm on textiles, 80 mm on the awning, 4 mm on printed menus as a bottom border. Never apply it to the logo, the walls, the floor, or digital backgrounds. The rule is: if a tourist can touch it, checker. Otherwise, no.

15 · Signage

Hand-lettered, gold leaf

One sign, above the door. "NONNA'S 12" in 15th-century Tuscan sign-painter style, gold leaf on black cypress wood. No neon, no backlit plastic, no logo stencil. Pay the sign-painter €480 and never touch it again.

Supplier

Bottega del Doratore, Via dei Servi 21, Firenze. Master Marco Benedetti has been hand-painting restaurant signs for 34 years. He does one sign a week. Book 3 weeks in advance. €480 all-in including the gold leaf and installation.

Window decal

Hours and booking QR code in cream on terracotta vinyl, bottom-right corner of the main window. 18×12 cm. €35 from any Florentine print shop.

16 · Packaging

Nothing leaves in a box

NONNA'S 12 has no takeaway. No delivery. No packaging. This is the one rule you never break. If you package pasta, you become a pasta shop. You are not a pasta shop — you are twelve seats.

Exception: the dolce jar

One small 220 ml kilner jar of tiramisu or panna cotta that a guest can take home after dinner for €6. Printed label: "Portami a casa — Nonna." That's the only thing that leaves the building. Margin is 78 %. About 30 % of guests buy one.

Takeaway class material

Pasta class guests leave with: one muslin bag of "00" flour, one recipe card hand-numbered, one glass of grappa at the door. All three together cost €1.80 and make the €120 class feel like a gift.

17 · Uniform

Flour, linen, leather

The nonna wears a cream linen apron over a slate grey shirt. The server wears the same apron over a white shirt. Nothing more. No logo patches, no caps, no gloves. Flour on the apron is the uniform — it means the pasta was rolled today.

18 · Menu & Pricing

Three dishes, one price

The menu is a single chalkboard rewritten every morning by the nonna. Three dishes. Two pastas, one dolce. Every dish is €14. Every dolce is €6. A half-litre of house Chianti is €9. That's the whole menu. It fits on a postcard.
