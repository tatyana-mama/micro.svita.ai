The Concept

200 pieces,
200 stories.

Berlin has vintage, but it's either €1 Humana kilo-rails or €300 Andreas Murkudis archival pieces. The middle — a €40–€120 Y2K find with real provenance — is missing.

BVS fills that gap with a deliberately small inventory: 200 pieces on the rack, nothing on the floor, every tag hand-typed with the decade, silhouette, fabric, and source. When a piece sells, it's replaced from the back stock the same evening — never an empty hanger.

The 200 Rule

Never more than 200 hangers on the rack. Every Wednesday morning, 30 new pieces come in, 30 go to the Monday kilo-rail at 50% for overflow. Scarcity is the product.

USPs & Positioning

Why this wins.

Five defensible advantages over both the Humana kilo-rails and the Mitte archive boutiques.

  1. 200 pieces, never more. Scarcity on the rack drives same-day decisions. No dig piles, no fatigue.
  2. Every tag hand-typed. Decade, silhouette, fabric, source — every piece has a story the customer can photograph for Depop resale.
  3. Wednesday drops. 30 new pieces every Wednesday at 14:00 — a weekly event that fills the shop without ads.
  4. RAW-Gelände rent. €850/month for 32 m² in the old railway depot — 50% below Simon-Dach prices.
  5. Wed–Sun only. Closed Mondays for sourcing trips, closed Tuesdays for inventory. Full opening hours only when customers actually shop.

Pattern Library

The tag.

One monogram, one tag, zero decorative pattern. Concrete floor + cream walls + chrome rack = pattern enough. The only repeating mark is the numbered tag.

Tag is used at its actual size on every piece (60×90 mm), never scaled. Numbered sequentially from 001 each week. Retired tags are archived in a cream box by the till.

Lighting Plan

High CRI, retail grade.

Vintage fabric colours must read true. The shop should feel like a daylit studio, not a Zara. Track spots over the rack, a warm glow over the till, nothing on a dimmer.
