The Concept

Hand-rolled,
kettle-boiled,
wood-fired.

Berlin has bagels, but they're frozen Polish imports reheated in a microwave. There is nothing in between the €1.20 gas-station pretzel and the €8 Mitte avocado-toast brunch. A proper New York bagel — rolled, boiled, fired — sold at €3.80 plain, €6.50 with schmear, served by 7 am.

Berlin Bagel Co fills that gap with 12 varieties on a fixed rotation, the same Hudson Valley flour used at Brooklyn's Shelsky's, and a Maurice wood-fired steam oven imported from Lyon. The format is small (22 m²) because Bergmannkiez rents are Kreuzberg-brutal.

The 4 AM Rule

Every morning at 4 am the dough is cold-retarded from yesterday, rolled by hand (no machine rollers, ever), kettle-boiled with honey-malt water for 90 seconds per side, then baked at 245°C until the crust cracks. First basket on the counter at 6:30. No second bake — if it's sold out by 14:00, it's sold out.

USPs & Positioning

Why this wins.

Five defensible advantages over the frozen-import bakeries and the Mitte avo-toast brunch spots.

  1. Kettle-boiled in honey-malt water. Customers watch the 60 L copper kettle going at 7 am through the window. No other Berlin bagelry does this step.
  2. Hudson Valley high-gluten flour. Same sack Shelsky's buys. Never a German Type 812 substitute.
  3. 12 varieties, no rotation. Menu cost control is total. Staff trains in 3 days, not 3 weeks.
  4. 22 m² footprint. Rent drops 40% below 30 m² in Bergmannkiez. Break-even gap that kills most Berlin bakery concepts is closed.
  5. Counter + 4 seats. No waiters, no table service, no VAT headache. Takeaway VAT 7%, eat-in 19%.

Pattern Library

BBC ring.

One monogram, one stamp, zero decorative pattern. The Bergmannkiez rule: if you can avoid a pattern, avoid it. Black shopfront + rye apron + mustard awning = pattern enough. The only repeating mark allowed is the bagel-ring stamp.

Monogram is used at 25 mm on receipts + napkins, 60 mm on bags, 400 mm on the shopfront awning. Never stretched, never rotated, never placed on a photograph.
