The Concept

Smørrebrød,
served standing.

Copenhagen sit-down smørrebrød restaurants charge 180–260 DKK per piece and take 45 minutes. The office lunch crowd does not have 45 minutes, and the Netto supermarket smørrebrød is depressing. There is nothing in between — a proper, made-this-morning smørrebrød served fast, at a counter, for 70 DKK.

Købn Smørre fills that gap with a disciplined 8-item menu, a standing counter, and the same rye bread the fine-dining places buy from Meyers Bageri. The format is deliberately tiny (18 m²) because real estate is the killer: rent costs drop 60% once you drop below 25 m² in Copenhagen.

The 90-Second Rule

From order to plate, every piece must be built and handed over in under 90 seconds. That means: toppings pre-portioned at 6am, rye sliced by machine, no compound assemblies, no table service. The kitchen runs like a Japanese yakitori stand — one motion per piece.

USPs & Positioning

Why this wins.

Five defensible advantages over both the sit-down smørrebrød restaurants and the supermarket shelf.

  1. Under 90 seconds, every time. Office workers cannot spend 45 minutes on lunch. Nobody else serves proper smørrebrød at this speed.
  2. Meyers Bageri rye bread. Same supplier used by the €200-per-head Michelin-starred places, served at a tenth the price.
  3. 8 items, no modifications. Menu cost control is total. Staff training takes three days, not three weeks.
  4. 18 m² footprint. Rent drops 60% below 25 m². Closes the Copenhagen break-even gap that kills most lunch concepts.
  5. Standing-only, cash discipline. No waiters, no tips, no table-turn anxiety. 60% labor saving vs a sit-down.

Pattern Library

No pattern.

The Danish brand rule: if you can avoid a pattern, avoid it. Cream wall + dusty blue panel + brass detail = pattern enough. The only repeating marks allowed are the monogram stamp (KS) and the numbered table of contents rules.

Monogram is used at 25 mm on paper (business cards, menu header, napkin embroidery), 80 mm in enamel (door pull), and 400 mm in hand-painted signage (shopfront). Never stretched, never rotated, never placed on a photograph.

Registration · Denmark

Legal setup in 2 weeks.

Denmark has Europe's cleanest sole-trader registration — one portal, one number, one fee. Add food hygiene and you are legal in under 14 days.

1. CVR Registration

  • Legal form: Enkeltmandsvirksomhed (sole trader)
  • Portal: virk.dk → "Start virksomhed"
  • Fee: 0 kr (free for Enkeltmandsvirksomhed)
  • Time: same-day, CVR number issued instantly
  • Required: NemID / MitID, CPR number

2. Skat (Tax)

  • Moms (VAT) number: automatic after CVR
  • VAT rate: 25% (no reduced rate for food in DK)
  • Income tax: Skat.dk, B-skat self-paid quarterly
  • Threshold: register for moms if turnover > 50 000 kr/yr

3. Fødevarekontrol

  • Register with Fødevarestyrelsen (foedevarestyrelsen.dk)
  • Elite-smiley scheme: inspection visible to public
  • HACCP / Egenkontrol plan required
  • First inspection: within 3 months

4. Insurance

  • No mandatory state social insurance for sole traders
  • Mandatory: arbejdsskadeforsikring if you hire
  • Recommended: erhvervsansvar (liability) ~€20/mo
  • Recommended: sygedagpenge-forsikring (sick pay)

Total legal + registration costs: €280 (CVR free, hygiene plan review ~€120, insurance first quarter ~€160)
