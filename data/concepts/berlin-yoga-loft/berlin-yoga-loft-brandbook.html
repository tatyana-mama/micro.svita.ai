The Concept

One room.
Warm from below.

Berlin yoga studios are either €20 drop-in chains in basement rooms with carpet, or €140/month Mitte boutiques that feel like spas. There is no top-floor Prenzlauer Berg loft, heated through the floor with infrared carbon panels, that sells a single 10-class pass for €150 and stays open until 21:00.

BYL fills that gap with one practice room, 56 m², eight mats, four classes a day (07:00 hatha, 09:30 vinyasa, 17:30 yin, 19:00 ashtanga). Infrared floor is on a timer: pre-heat 20 min, cool 10 min after class. No changing rooms — bring a tote, leave it on the shelf.

The 8-Mat Rule

Never more than 8 mats in a class. Capacity is the product — the room is not maximised for revenue, it's maximised for attention. When a class is full, it closes on the app. No add-ons, no standing at the back.

USPs & Positioning

Why this wins.

  1. Infrared floor, 28°C at practice start. No hot-room fans, no humidity, no claustrophobic basement. Warmth from below, natural light from above.
  2. Eight mats, never nine. Capacity is the brand. Teachers correct alignment hands-on.
  3. Top-floor loft, 3.2 m ceiling. Prenzlauer Berg has dozens of basement studios. Light and height are the differentiator.
  4. 10-pack €150 or nothing. No drop-in, no monthly, no commitment gym-trap. Pre-paid, 6-month expiry.
  5. Four classes, four styles, same room. Hatha 07:00, vinyasa 09:30, yin 17:30, ashtanga 19:00. Teachers rotate, format is fixed.
