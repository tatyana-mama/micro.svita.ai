The Concept

Twelve flavours.
One cube. No mercy.

Italian gelato is a crowded market dominated by two archetypes: the tourist trap (50 flavours, neon colours) and the old family gelateria (8 flavours, 30 years, no social media). Milano Gelato Cube takes the family discipline and gives it a new ritual — the Monday rewrite. Twelve is what fits in one Bravo Trittico per day, twelve is what a customer can read in 90 seconds, twelve is what a chalkboard can hold with dignity.

The walk-up cube model skips the #1 gelateria trap: committing €25,000 to a full retail fitout. Every asset in this brandbook is physically movable or paintable-over. The cube is the logo. The chalkboard is the menu. The barista is the service.

The Twelve Rule

Never 11, never 13. Whatever isn't sold by closing goes home with the team or to Banco Alimentare — never to tomorrow. This is the discipline that makes the brand.

USPs & Positioning

Why this wins.

Five defensible advantages over the average Milanese gelateria.

  1. Exactly twelve flavours. Never more. The constraint is the story — and the chalkboard is the discipline.
  2. Churned this morning, gone tomorrow. Every cone from a Bravo Trittico batch made within 24 hours. No yesterday.
  3. Weekly Monday rotation. 4 permanent + 8 rotating. Regulars get a ritual; competitors with 30-pan cabinets can't replicate the cadence.
  4. Walk-up cube, no seating. Zero turnover drama, zero cleanup staff, zero table service. The line is the shop.
  5. €9,400 to open, 5 weeks start to first cone. Modular cube = no wall renovation, no plumbing commitment to a leased space.

For Milanesi who treat gelato as a weekly ritual rather than an occasional treat, Milano Gelato Cube is the 10 m² walk-up window whose 12-flavour weekly rotation turns a scoop into a standing appointment with Monday morning.

Pattern Library

The 12-dot grid.

The brand pattern is a single idea: twelve circles in a 4×3 grid, each circle a flavour. Used on sleeves, receipts, the floor decal at the window, and the Instagram grid marker. Never more than twelve dots. Never fewer.

Rules

4 columns × 3 rows. Circle diameter = 6% of surface width. Space between circles = ½ diameter. Colours sampled from actual flavours of the week. Never a perfect grid — Monday's hand-placement wobble is part of the brand.

Registration · Italy

Legal setup in 10 days.

Italy is famously bureaucratic, but a ditta individuale (sole trader) for food retail in Milano runs through SUAP and Camera di Commercio within two business weeks. Total legal spend: €550.

1. Ditta individuale

  • Legal form: Ditta individuale
  • Portal: registroimprese.it
  • Camera di Commercio Milano fee: €88 + €25/year
  • Time: 5 business days
  • Required: codice fiscale + passport + PEC email

2. Partita IVA + regime

  • P.IVA: free at Agenzia delle Entrate, 24 h
  • Regime forfettario: 5% tax first 5 years (<€85k)
  • Codice ATECO: 56.10.30 (gelaterie)
  • IVA on gelato: 10% (food takeaway)

3. SUAP + SCIA Milano

  • SCIA food retail filed via SUAP: €160
  • HACCP self-control plan (€80 template)
  • ATS Milano hygiene visit within 30 d
  • Insegna di esercizio (sign permit): €120

4. INPS + Insurance

  • INPS gestione commercianti: ~€4,200/year
  • INAIL (accident): mandatory, ~€180/year
  • RC terzi (liability): €180/year
  • Dipendenti (if hiring): consulente del lavoro €90/mo

Total legal + registration costs: ~€550 (Camera di Commercio €88 + SCIA €160 + sign permit €120 + HACCP template €80 + misc filings €100). INPS is an annual running cost, not setup.
